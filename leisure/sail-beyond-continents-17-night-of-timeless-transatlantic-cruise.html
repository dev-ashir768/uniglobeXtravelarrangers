Sail Beyond Continents: 17 Night of Timeless Transatlantic Cruise

A transatlantic cruise offers a timeless journey where you can relax to the soothing rhythm of the waves, enjoy breathtaking sunrises and sunsets, and indulge in elegant evenings. Connect with the sea while immersing yourself in vibrant onboard life, from gourmet dining to captivating entertainment. Each voyage is enhanced by thrilling port experiences, whether exploring historic European cities or lounging on pristine pink sand beaches, inviting you to savor the beauty of the ocean and the rich cultures that await on both sides of the Atlantic.

Itinerary:

Day 1: Mon, Mar 30, 2026 Buenos Aires, Argentina

Buenos Aires pulses with creativity and culture. This vibrant city blends Native, Spanish, Italian, French, and British influences—welcoming you with a warm kiss from the locals, known as Porteños. Explore 300 theaters and 160 museums, like the elegant Teatro Colón or Museo Nacional de Arte Decorativo. Shop designer finds and local crafts at Galerías Pacifico and along lively Calle Florida. From historic bars to trendy cafés, Buenos Aires invites you to eat, drink, and experience its rich charm.

Day 2-3: Tue, Mar 31, 2026 - Wed, Apr 1, 2026 2

Days at Sea 

Day 4: Thu, Apr 2, 2026 Rio De Janeiro, Brazil

Rio de Janeiro dazzles with dramatic landscapes, vibrant beach culture, and the world-famous Carnival. Ride a cable car to Sugarloaf or hike Corcovado for stunning views of Guanabara Bay. Soak up the sun at iconic beaches like Copacabana and Ipanema, or explore Brazil’s rich history at the Museu Historico Nacional.

Day 5: Fri, Apr 3, 2026 

Day at Sea

Day 6: Sat, Apr 4, 2026 Salvador de Bahia, Brazil

Salvador, the capital of Bahia and Brazil’s fourth-largest city, blends rich colonial history with vibrant Afro-Brazilian culture. Its UNESCO-listed Pelourinho district showcases colorful architecture and over 300 churches, including the ornate São Francisco. Music, dance, and street festivals fill the air with energy, while capoeira performances reflect deep cultural roots. Just beyond the city, Bahia’s stunning Atlantic beaches offer the perfect coastal escape.

Day 7 - 12 : Sun, Apr 5, 2026 - Fri, Apr 10, 2026 

6 Days at Sea

Day 13: Sat, Apr 11, 2026 Tenerife, Canary Islands

Tenerife, the largest of the Canary Islands, offers a striking mix of natural beauty and cultural gems. Explore the mysterious pyramids of Güímar, see exotic wildlife at Loro Park, or catch a performance at the sleek Tenerife Auditorium. Food lovers can savor fresh seafood, local cheeses, and fine island wines. The island’s landscape shifts dramatically—from the lush, rain-kissed north to the sun-drenched, arid south—offering a unique experience wherever you go.

Day 14: Sun, Apr 12, 2026 

Day at Sea

Day 15: Mon, Apr 13, 2026 Seville (Cadiz), Spain 

From Cádiz you can easily get to Seville, exploring the old town with its quaint Moorish feel as well as its impressive monuments, pretty parks and lovely architecture. Along the way, restore your energy at any of the mouth-watering tapas bars offering some of the country’s finest seafood and typical Andalusian cuisine.

Day 16: Tue, Apr 14, 2026 Malaga, Spain

Malaga is the gateway to the Andalusian countryside and the Costa del Sol, some of the region's most impressive beaches. It also is home to a number of magnificent palaces, including the Moorish Alcabaza and Gibralfaro.

Day 17: Wed, Apr 15, 2026 

Day at Sea

Day 18: Thu, Apr 16, 2026 Barcelona, Spain 

Once a quiet Spanish town, Barcelona is now a vibrant blend of medieval charm and modern flair. Explore 2,000 years of architecture—from Roman ruins to Gaudí’s masterpieces—and world-class art museums featuring Picasso, Dalí, Miró, and more. Unwind by the Mediterranean, hike the Collserola Hills, or visit Montjuïc before diving into the city’s legendary nightlife.
